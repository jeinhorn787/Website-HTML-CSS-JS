Dubstep: The Artists

Legendary Classics and New Age Producers and Their Hit Songs

The Pioneers/Legends

These artists have had massive influence on the dubstep scene for a while, whether they are still active or not. There are too many to choose from, but here are some top notch producers.

  • Skrillex

    Image Source: edmsauce.com

    Need we say anything about this man? Sonny John Moore, A.K.A. Skrillex, is credited with the start of the dubstep revolution in the United States. His songs captivated the world almost instantly after the release of his Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites EP.
    Song: Scary Monsters and Nice Sprites


  • Skream

    Image Source: 6am-group.com

    Considered to be one of the founding father's of dubstep, Oliver Dene Jones, A.K.A Skream, is an international sensation. While he does not participate in dubstep anymore, the community will never forget what this man did for the scene.
    Song: Exothermic Reaction


  • Benga

    Image Source: planet.mu

    Skream's partner in crime, Adegbenga Adejumo, A.K.A Benga, helped evolved dubstep from the ground up, being one of the earliest pioneers.
    Song: I Will Never Change


  • Emalkay

    Image Source: bassmusic.wikia.com

    Martin Knowles, A.K.A Emalkay, has been active since the early days of dubstep. His songs reached mass amounts of people, and for good reason!
    Song: When I Look At You


  • Trolley Snatcha

    Image Source: 24ourmusic.net

    Still extremely active in the scene and with a strong following, Zack Kemp, A.K.A Trolley Snatcha, has been pushing the sound of dubstep furhter and further with every release.
    Song: Make My Whole World


  • Doctor P

    Image Source: destinyevents.ca

    A name that even non-dubstep fans may know, Shaun Brockhurst, A.K.A Doctor P, has some internationally acclaimed songs. Known for his unique high-pitched basses, it is obvious why audiences were very interested in his sounds.
    Song: Sweet Shop


  • Excision

    Image Source: wegotthiscovered.com

    Starting out in the late 2000's, Jeff Abel, A.K.A Excision, has one of the largest followings in dubstep currently. He tours the world with his game changing stage production and created the first dubstep/bass music festival in the US.
    Song: The Paradox



New Age Artists

Ever since dubstep had blown-up worldwide, more and more people jumped on board and tried their hand at dubstep. Everyone in this list is still active and has been pushing the scene to new limits everyday.

  • Badklaat

    Image Source: bassrush.com

    Known for his aggresive sounds, Adam James, A.K.A Badklaat, became quickly popular in 2016-2017 as his songs energize the venue to a new level.
    Song: Head Crush


  • Virtual Riot

    Image Source: owsla.com

    Christian Valentin Brunn, A.K.A Virtual Riot, is well known for being a musical genius as he knows a lot about music theory. His mix of melodical yet heavy dubstep is the perfect blend which leads to one of the most loyal followings in dubstep.
    Song: Beyond


  • Barely Alive

    Image Source: partyflock.nl

    Known for always wearing the signature helmet on stage, Matt Meier and Willie Watkins, A.K.A Barely Alive, exploded onto the scene in 2016-2017 with their very new age style sounds. Their theme revolves around the technological revolution and how we have become used to it... maybe TOO used to it?
    Song: Elephant


  • Svdden Death

    Image Source: Svdden Death's Twitter

    One of the quickest uprisings in dubstep history, Danny Howland, A.K.A Svdden Death, brings a whole new vibe to dubstep that the masses are eating up. While lots of dubstep producers are making their songs at the tempo of 150 bpm, Svdden Death sticks to the classic 140 bpm and makes it sound like new.
    Song: Junkworld

  • LAXX

    Image Source: beatport.com

    Never Say Die Records brought on James Churchill, A.K.A LAXX, around 2015 and it one of the best moves for the label. His unique sound and powerful songs make him a household name, playing shows internationally.
    Song: Step Two


  • Rickyxsan

    Image Source: edmtunes.com

    Rickyxsan is not bound to just dubstep or just one other genre. He is a man of many talents who can make music that will get the venue moving. He also has created some soft, beautiful melodic tunes so this guy knows exactly what he is doing.
    Song: Nu Dub


  • Trampa

    Image Source: sightsandsounds.com

    The wildly aggressive and energetic sounds of Rikki Moore's, A.K.A Trampa, songs are extremely well known in the dubstep scene. Cities across the world demand that he play a show at their city, as they know exactly what kind of energy he can bring.
    Song: Bulldozer
